## Bangladesh's Gambling Laws: From 1867 to Today

The foundation of Bangladesh's gambling law is the **Public Gambling Act of 1967**. This law came from the Pakistan era, and independent Bangladesh has not abolished it. The law primarily regulates offline gambling.

Section 3 of this Act states that gambling in a public place or at a public gathering is an offense. Here are several key points to note:

**First**, the law mentions "public place" and "public gambling." This means if someone gambles at a shopping mall, restaurant, or public gathering, it falls under the law.

**Second**, the law holds both "organizers" and "participants" accountable. This means not just the organizer of gambling, but the player as well.

**Third**, there are some exceptions in the law. Some leniency is given to betting associated with licensed sports like horse racing.

But here's the main problem. When this law was enacted in 1967, there were no computers, no internet, no smartphones. The words "online gambling" or "online casino" do not appear anywhere in this law. This is the legal vacuum where many people interpret things according to their own understanding.

**Offline gambling** — such as card games, lotteries, or gambling dens — falls directly under the Public Gambling Act. Police can directly raid such activities. Both participants and organizers of such gambling can face legal problems.
